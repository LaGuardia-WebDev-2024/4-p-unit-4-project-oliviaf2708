New York Transit Museum

The New York Transit Museum is located inside a decommissioned subway station at 99
Schermerhorn Street. It exhibits the long history of NYC public transportation. Inside
of the museum, you can find vintage subway cars, turnstiles, buses, and displays of transit
engineering and history. Visitors are able to walk through, sit in, and view these pieces
of New York History. There are also special "Nostalgia Rides" on vintage trains that are
ticketed sperearly from regualr entry.



Brooklyn Heights Promenade

The Brooklyn Heights Promenade is a walkway overlooking beautiful views of the Manhattan
Skyline, New York Harbor, and the Brooklyn Bridge. Built in the 1950s, its purpose was to cover
the BQE out of preservation for the neighborhood, but it now serves as a beautiful, quiet
and atmospheric walk lined with historic townhouses, benches and trees. It also has a close
proximity to Brooklyn Bridge Park, and a perfectly unobstructed view of the city. It is super
popular for anything from a nice stroll to rollerblading.



The River Cafe

The River Cafe is a delicious New American fine dining restaurant located under the Brooklyn
Bridge. Known for its romantic atmosphere and Skyline views, the establishment serves seasonal
dishes, and has won awards such as a Michelin star and a Wine Spectator Award of Excellence.
The service is attentive, the ingredience are high quality, and the experience is amazing. Reservations are
typically suggested as this restaurant is very popular.
